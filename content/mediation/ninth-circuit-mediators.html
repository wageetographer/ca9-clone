The Ninth Circuit Mediation Office is led by Chief Circuit Mediator Stephen M. Liacouras and a team of seven Circuit Mediators who work exclusively for the court of appeals. The Circuit Mediators are all licensed attorneys who have an average of twenty-five years of combined government, private-law and mediation practice. They are all experienced and highly trained in appellate mediation, negotiation, and Ninth Circuit practice and procedure. The Circuit Mediators are generalists and cases are assigned to them across all subject matter.

Roxane Ashe, Circuit Mediator

Roxane has been a Circuit Mediator for Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals since 1998 and has mediated over two thousand appellate cases. Roxane is also the principal mediator for employment matters brought under the Circuit's Employment Dispute Resolution Plan and serves as a panel mediator for the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California. Prior to joining the court’s mediation office, Roxane was Supervising Ninth Circuit Motions Attorney, Assistant Circuit Executive for Legal Affairs, and practiced civil litigation at a large firm in St. Louis, Missouri. She earned her J.D. and B.A from the University of Tennessee. Originally from Nashville, Roxane lives by the ocean in Half Moon Bay, California.

Sasha M. Cummings, Circuit Mediator

Sasha joined the court with 20 years of experience as a partner at a national law firm, trial attorney, and mediator. While in private practice, she served extensively as a Judge Pro Tem, Settlement Conference Officer, and Mandatory Settlement Conference Panelist. She is an Adjunct Professor at USF School of Law and served on the UC Hastings Foundation Board of Trustees. Raised in Northern California’s Wine Country, Sasha earned her J.D. from UC Hastings Law and her B.A. from Wesleyan University in Connecticut.

Chris Goelz, Circuit Mediator

Chris has been a Circuit Mediator for the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals for 30 years. Before joining the court’s mediation office, he was a Motions Attorney for the Ninth Circuit. Prior to coming to the court Chris was a civil litigator in a small San Francisco firm. Chris has taught negotiation as an Adjunct Professor at the University of Washington School of Law and is an author of a Ninth Circuit practice guide published by the Rutter Group. Chris went to Yale Law School and then clerked for Judge Jonathan Newman of the Oregon Court of Appeal. Chris grew up in Half Moon Bay, California and has an undergraduate degree in mechanical engineering from the University of California, Santa Barbara. Before attending law school, he worked as a production engineer in Silicon Valley.

Bob Kaiser, Circuit Mediator

Prior to joining the Ninth Circuit mediation office, Bob mediated federal cases as a Circuit Mediator for the Sixth Circuit Court Appeals and also as a mediator for the U.S. District Court for the Southern District of Ohio. Bob has also mediated hundreds of cases as a private mediator. Before devoting his full-time efforts to mediation, Bob practiced law for 20 years, litigating and trying a wide variety of civil cases. Originally from Ohio, Bob received his J.D. from the University of Cincinnati College of Law and his B.G.S. from the University of Cincinnati.

Stephen M. Liacouras, Chief Circuit Mediator

Stephen was selected as Chief Circuit Mediator in 2018, after having served as a Circuit Mediator for 17 years. Prior to joining the mediation office, he practiced civil litigation at small San Francisco firm, served as a Supervising Ninth Circuit Staff Attorney, and worked as an in-house attorney for a technology company in Austin, Texas. Stephen teaches a negotiation class as an Adjunct Professor at UC Hastings Law and is a volunteer panel mediator for the U.S. District Court for Northern District of California. He received his J.D. from Yale Law School and then clerked for Chief Judge Dolores Sloviter on the Third Circuit Court of Appeals. Originally from Philadelphia, Stephen got his B.A. in History from the University of California at Berkeley.

Steve Saltiel, Circuit Mediator

Kyungah “Kay” Suk, Circuit Mediator
